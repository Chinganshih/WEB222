Let's learn Blue Jay - Food&Nesting!

Nesting:

Blue Jay builds an open cup of grass, mud, and twigs in the crotch and both male and female gather their nesting materials.
There are some nesting facts. The size of clutch is around 2-7eggs. The number of broods is one brood. The length of egg is about 1.0-1.3 in.
The width of egg is about 0.7-0.9 in. The period of incubation is 17-18 days. The period of nesting is 17-21 days.

Food:

Wild fruits, acorns, hazelnuts, beechnuts, corn, and other grains, and also insects are Blue Jay’s diet.
Sometimes, Blue Jays pick up dead birds or take nuts and seeds from trees or ground.
They store food and hide it to consume later.
The way of Blue Jays holding their food is to hold the hull of a seed or peanut beneath their feet while pecking.
