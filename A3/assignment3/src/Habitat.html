Let's learn Blue Jay - Habitat

Habitat:

Oaks, forests, woodlots, towns, cities, and parks are all good places to find acorns, which are a favorite food of Blue Jays.
The blue jay is found in many types of forests but is especially plentiful near oak trees.
Their range extends from southern Canada to Texas and Florida.
Especially in northern parts of its range, this species is partially migratory.
They are more abundant along forest edges than in deep forests.
Oak trees are common in urban and suburban areas where they live.
Also, Blue Jays breeds in the forests of southern Manitoba , central Alberta, Saskatchewan, central and southern Ontario to southern Quebec, the island of Newfoundland, New Brunswick, Prince Edward Island, and Nova Scotia.
