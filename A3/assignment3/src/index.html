How size is Blue Jay!

  • Weight: 2.5-3.5 oz (70-100 g)
  • Wingspan: 13.4-16.9 in (34-43 cm)

The fact of Blue Jay!

Blue Jays have a complex social structure and tight family ties, and they are known for their intelligence.
Jays are also called jaybirds because of their noise; it has been applied to other birds in the same family that are mostly gregarious.
Blue jay (Cyanocitta cristata) is a bird in the family Corvidae found in eastern North America.
It is found primarily in eastern and central United States.
Some eastern populations may migrate. There are also resident populations in Newfoundland, Canada; breeding populations are found throughout southern Canada.
Both deciduous and coniferous forests are breeding grounds, and it is common in suburban areas.
This animal's coloration is primarily blue, with a white chest and underparts, as well as a blue crest.
Its neck is covered with a U-shaped collar, with a black border around it.
Females and males are similar in appearance, and their plumage doesn't change during the year.
Several subspecies are known. A blue jay prefers seeds and nuts, such as acorns, soft fruits, arthropods, and small vertebrates.
Typically, it eats from trees, shrubs, and the ground, and sometimes it hunts insects.
Other birds have been decapitated by blue jays; they sometimes raid nests and raid nests of other birds.
